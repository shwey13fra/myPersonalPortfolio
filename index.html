My Story

"I didn't set out to become a product manager. The path found me through curiosity."

Safety-critical
engineering
Shipping
user products
PM
thinking

My career began in ADAS — building, testing, and shipping features for safety-critical automotive systems. Working at that level of consequence gave me something most product people don't start with: a visceral understanding of how software behaves when real users depend on it, in conditions where failure isn't an option.

But the more I shipped, the more I found myself drawn upstream. Why were we building this feature? What problem were we actually solving? Were we solving the right one? I started stepping into requirement discussions, trade-off conversations, and prioritization calls — especially when user safety and experience were at stake. I wasn't just executing anymore. I was questioning.

I discovered I was most engaged when bringing structure to ambiguous problems, aligning stakeholders around a shared direction, and ensuring the thing we were building was worth building. That's product management — and it's where my technical depth, user empathy, and instinct for decision-making finally found a home.
